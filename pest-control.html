Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Sustainable pest monitoring, biological controls, and organic remedies for healthy crop yields[cite: 1].

Core IPM Control Pillars

Combine multiple management practices to control pest populations safely[cite: 1].

Biological Controls

Introduce natural predators (ladybugs, parasitic wasps, lacewings) and beneficial microbes to keep pest populations in check[cite: 1].

Physical Barriers

Use insect netting, sticky traps, mulching, and soil solarization to physically prevent pests from reaching crops[cite: 1].

Organic Remedies

Apply eco-friendly sprays such as neem oil, insecticidal soaps, and horticultural oils that minimize environmental impact[cite: 1].

Early Detection & Crop Monitoring Tips

Early detection is critical to stopping pest outbreaks before they cause severe economic damage[cite: 1].

Inspect crops at least twice a week during active growing seasons[cite: 1]. Check leaf undersides, stem joints, and soil lines for eggs, larvae, or feeding damage[cite: 1].

Hang yellow sticky traps at crop height to track airborne insects like whiteflies and aphids[cite: 1]. Use pheromone traps to monitor adult moth activity[cite: 1].

Do not spray at the sight of a single insect[cite: 1]. Apply remedies only when pest population levels exceed economic threshold limits[cite: 1].

Common Pests & Treatment Matrix

Reference guide for identifying pest symptoms and applying targeted treatments[cite: 1].

Pest Name Target Crops Symptoms / Damage Recommended Remedy
Aphids Vegetables, Fruits, Cereals Curled leaves, sticky honeydew buildup, stunted growth Neem oil spray, introducing Ladybugs (biological)
Fall Armyworm Maize, Wheat, Rice Large ragged holes in leaves, sawdust-like frass on whorls Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) spray, pheromone trapping
Whiteflies Tomato, Cotton, Beans Yellowing leaves, transmission of viral plant diseases Yellow sticky traps, insecticidal soaps
Spider Mites Fruits, Vegetables, Ornamentals Fine webbing under leaves, yellow stippling on leaf surface Predatory mites (Phytoseiulus), overhead water washing
