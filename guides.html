Guide: Organic Soil Preparation & Composting
Overview & Objectives

Preparing healthy, organic-rich soil is the foundational step for sustainable farming[cite: 1]. Follow these step-by-step practices to optimize soil texture and microbial activity[cite: 1].

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Soil Testing & Assessment: Measure existing soil pH and nutrient levels (target pH range: 6.0 to 7.0 for standard crops)[cite: 1].
  2. Land Clearing & Tillage: Aerate topsoil up to 8-10 inches without disturbing deeper microbial layers[cite: 1].
  3. Organic Matter Incorporation: Spread 2-3 inches of well-rotted compost or vermicompost over the field[cite: 1].
  4. Cover Cropping: Plant nitrogen-fixing legumes (e.g., clover) during off-seasons to fix atmospheric nitrogen[cite: 1].
Best Practices & Pro-Tips
  • Avoid tilling wet soil to prevent compaction[cite: 1].
  • Maintain compost moisture levels at ~50% (feels like a damp wrung-out sponge)[cite: 1].
Guide: Setting Up a Drip Hydroponic System
Overview & Objectives

Hydroponic drip systems deliver nutrient solution directly to plant roots with minimal water waste[cite: 1]. Ideal for greenhouse environments and space-constrained farms[cite: 1].

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Reservoir & Pump Assembly: Place a clean water tank under the growing tray and connect a submersible pump to main supply lines[cite: 1].
  2. Plumbing & Drip Lines: Run main 1/2-inch tubing along plant rows and attach 1/4-inch micro-drip lines to each plant container[cite: 1].
  3. Substrate Preparation: Fill growing pots with inert medium (clay pebbles or coco coir) for physical root support[cite: 1].
  4. Nutrient Calibration: Mix water-soluble N-P-K nutrients; calibrate Electrical Conductivity (EC) between 1.2–2.0 mS/cm and pH between 5.5–6.5[cite: 1].
  5. Timer Integration: Set an automated timer to drip nutrient solution 3–5 times daily based on plant age[cite: 1].
Best Practices & Pro-Tips
  • Flush drip lines monthly with clean water to prevent mineral salt buildup[cite: 1].
  • Keep reservoir water temperature around 18°C–21°C (65°F–70°F) to prevent root rot[cite: 1].
Guide: Optimal Wheat Cultivation & Irrigation
Overview & Objectives

Maximize cereal yield through precise sowing techniques, critical irrigation timing, and targeted field management during the winter season[cite: 1].

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Field Preparation & Seed Treatment: Plow field thoroughly to achieve a fine seedbed. Treat certified seeds with fungicide before sowing[cite: 1].
  2. Precision Sowing: Sow seeds at a depth of 4–5 cm with row spacing of 20–22.5 cm during late autumn/early winter[cite: 1].
  3. Critical Irrigation Management: Apply the first critical irrigation at Crown Root Initiation (CRI) stage (20–25 days after sowing)[cite: 1].
  4. Nutrient Top-Dressing: Apply half-dose nitrogen basal during sowing, and top-dress remaining nitrogen after first irrigation[cite: 1].
  5. Harvesting: Harvest when grain moisture content drops below 14% and stems turn straw-golden brown[cite: 1].
Best Practices & Pro-Tips
  • Never miss the CRI stage irrigation, as it causes up to 30% yield loss[cite: 1].
  • Ensure dry grain storage to prevent rust and weevil infestation[cite: 1].
