Crop Profile: Rice (Oryza sativa)
Key Characteristics
  • Category: Grains & Cereals[cite: 1]
  • Major Varieties: Basmati, Jasmine, Indica, Japonica[cite: 1]
  • Ideal Soil Type: Clay or clay-loam with high water holding capacity[cite: 1]
  • Soil pH Range: 5.5 to 6.5[cite: 1]

Transplanted puddled conditions are standard for lowland rice[cite: 1]. Requires warm temperature (20°C–35°C) and flooded field conditions during early tillering stages[cite: 1].

Harvest when 80-85% of grains turn straw yellow[cite: 1]. Thresh, dry to 12-14% moisture[cite: 1]. Used for direct food consumption, flour, rice bran oil, and animal fodder[cite: 1].
Crop Profile: Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)
Key Characteristics
  • Category: Vegetables[cite: 1]
  • Major Varieties: Roma, Cherry, Beefsteak, Heirloom[cite: 1]
  • Ideal Soil Type: Well-drained sandy loam rich in organic matter[cite: 1]
  • Soil pH Range: 6.0 to 6.8[cite: 1]

Raised-bed planting with staking or trellising is recommended for indeterminate varieties[cite: 1]. Requires consistent drip irrigation to avoid blossom end rot[cite: 1].

Crop Profile: Mango (Mangifera indica)
Key Characteristics
  • Category: Fruits[cite: 1]
  • Major Varieties: Alphonso, Haden, Tommy Atkins, Kent[cite: 1]
  • Ideal Soil Type: Deep, well-drained alluvial or sandy loam[cite: 1]
  • Soil pH Range: 5.5 to 7.5[cite: 1]

Propagated through grafting (veneer or side grafting)[cite: 1]. Planted at 10m x 10m spacing[cite: 1]. Requires clear dry weather during flowering stage[cite: 1].

Crop Profile: Cotton (Gossypium)
Key Characteristics
  • Category: Cash Crops[cite: 1]
  • Major Varieties: Upland, Pima, Egyptian Long-Staple[cite: 1]
  • Ideal Soil Type: Deep black cotton soil or well-drained clay loam[cite: 1]
  • Soil pH Range: 6.0 to 8.0[cite: 1]

Sown in rows at 60–90 cm spacing[cite: 1]. Requires a long frost-free period, high sunshine hours, and moderate rainfall[cite: 1].
